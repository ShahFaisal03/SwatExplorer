Overview

Pakistan's Most Breathtaking Alpine Lake

Mahodand Lake is one of Pakistan's most spectacular natural wonders — a vast turquoise lake cradled at 2,800 metres above sea level, surrounded by towering snow-capped peaks, lush alpine meadows, and dense forests. The name "Mahodand" means "Lake of Fish" in the local Pashto dialect.

The lake stretches nearly 3 kilometres in length and is fed by glacial meltwater from the surrounding peaks, giving it its signature emerald-blue colour. In summer, the meadows around the lake burst into a kaleidoscope of wildflowers, and the air carries a cool crispness that feels a world away from city life.

Getting here is part of the adventure — a thrilling 4x4 jeep ride from Kalam through dramatic mountain scenery. The journey itself is unforgettable.

Lake Facts

Know Before You Go

🌡

Temperature

Even in peak summer (July), temperatures at the lake rarely exceed 18°C. Nights drop to near freezing — always bring warm clothing.

💧

Water Source

Fed entirely by glacial meltwater from surrounding peaks, giving the lake its signature turquoise-emerald colour and crystal clarity.

🐟

Fish Species

Home to an abundant population of rainbow and brown trout. Fishing is permitted with a licence — a dream destination for anglers.

🚙

Road Access

Accessible only by 4x4 jeep from Kalam. The 35 km jeep track takes 1.5–2 hours and is one of Pakistan's most scenic drives.

🌸

Best Months

June to September is ideal. July–August offers the most vibrant wildflower blooms and warmest daytime temperatures at the lake.

⛺

Camping

Camping is allowed on the meadows surrounding the lake. Basic tents and bedding are available for rent from local vendors near the shore.

Visitor Tips

Essential Tips for Mahodand

🧥

Dress Very WarmAt 2,800m, it can snow even in July. Pack a heavy jacket, gloves and thermal layers — even for day trips.

🚙

Jeep OnlyNever attempt the road to Mahodand in a regular car. Only hire experienced local jeep drivers who know the track well.

💵

No Facilities Up ThereNo shops, ATMs or restaurants at the lake. Bring all food, water, cash and medicine from Kalam before heading up.

🕗

Start EarlyLeave Kalam by 7–8 AM. Afternoon clouds can roll in quickly at altitude, so arrive early for the best views.

🌿

Respect the EnvironmentMahodand is pristine. Take all rubbish back with you — leave no trace. Keep the lake and meadows beautiful for everyone.

🐴

Horse Riding BargainingNegotiate horse ride prices before mounting. Standard rate is Rs 500–800 per person per round. Agree upfront.
